Presenting the Material

Social Function, Text Structure & Language Features

DikaI think students should be allowed to bring smartphones to school.
SariI don't think so. It can be distracting during lessons.
DikaWell, I see your point, but we can use them for research too.
SariThat's true. I agree, as long as teachers set clear rules.

Agreement and disagreement texts are used to state, ask about, and respond to someone's opinion — whether agreeing or disagreeing — while still maintaining politeness and a good social relationship between speakers. This function is important in class discussions, group work, and everyday conversation.

In general, an agreement/disagreement dialogue follows this pattern:

  • Opinion — the speaker states an initial opinion. "I think..."
  • Response — the listener responds, agreeing or disagreeing.
  • Reason — the reason supporting that response.
  • Closing / Follow-up — closing or continuing the discussion.

✔ Agreement

I agree.
A simple, direct way to agree.
I couldn't agree more.
A strong way to show full agreement.
That's exactly what I think.
Shows your opinion matches exactly.
You have a point there.
A polite way to accept part of an idea.

✘ Disagreement

I don't think so.
A mild, everyday way to disagree.
I'm afraid I disagree.
A polite, softened way to disagree.
I see it differently.
Shows a different point of view.
Well, I'm not so sure.
Shows hesitation or doubt.

    Summary

    Wrapping Up & Reflecting

    Key Points

    • Agreement/disagreement texts function to express and respond to opinions politely.
    • Structure: Opinion → Response → Reason → Closing.
    • Agreement expressions: I agree, Exactly, You have a point, etc.
    • Disagreement expressions: I don't think so, I'm afraid I disagree, etc.
    • Polite language matters so discussions stay healthy, even when opinions differ.
